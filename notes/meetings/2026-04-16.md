## Key points

- **Baseline candidate: LongVideoAgent** — Su flags it as the most pragmatic
  starting point because it ships both training and evaluation code/data.
- **Framing of the field:** existing long-video agent methods collapse into
  two questions — *when to look* (frame selection / keyframe retrieval) and
  *how to look* (per-frame querying, zoom, cropping, multi-turn interrogation).
- **Su's mapping of the surveyed papers:**
  - *When to look:* **LongVideoAgent** — visual sampling.
  - *How to look:*
    - **VideoSeek** — granularity control
    - **GCA** — geometric understanding
    - **RieMind** — graph-based reasoning
    - **VADAR** — code-based planning
    - **VideoThinker** — caption-style chain-of-thought
    - **AoTD** — thought distillation
    - **ReAgent-V** — self-reflection
    - **LVAgent** — agent collaboration
- **Shared assumption Su wants to challenge:** every method above is trying
  to make the agent exploit *more* frames. Humans reason stably from far
  fewer moments (eye ≈ 60 FPS effective), which motivates the opposite
  direction.

## Su's proposal

- **Goal:** match 60-frame performance with 30-frame input.
- **Mechanism:** *implicit* compression — not dropping or summarizing frames,
  but training the model to predict the full 60-frame content from the
  30-frame subset.
- **Analogy:** similar in form to world generation models, but the intent is
  *internalization* of world state rather than explicit generation.
- **Training signal:** auxiliary loss on the generation side, so the encoder
  is pressured to develop a deeper world model.
- **Concrete path:** modify LongVideoAgent's existing pipeline rather than
  building something new, since it already provides end-to-end training +
  eval infrastructure.
