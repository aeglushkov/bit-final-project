## Key points

- **VSI-Bench is a starting point, not the goal.** The "Thinking in Space"
  repo is task-definition + eval scripts; there's nothing to "reproduce" in
  the conventional sense. The actual research direction is **Agentic AI +
  multimodal understanding**.
- **Empirical motivation (from Su's own tests):** VLMs answer correctly
  given *video + question*, but become inconsistent when given a *caption*
  of the same video + question. Reads as evidence that VLMs are pattern-
  matching on pixels, not genuinely reasoning about space.
- **Su's diagnosis:** current video MLLMs align modalities data-drivenly
  without explicit spatial reasoning — the latent capability is there but
  not invoked.
- **Proposed direction:** an **agent layer on top of SOTA VLMs** that acts
  as a reasoning coordinator, not a black-box caller.
  - Decomposes complex spatial tasks into sub-problems
  - Conducts step-by-step reasoning externally
  - Queries the VLM multiple times, targeting specific frames / regions
  - Integrates visual cues across frames and viewpoints
  - Guides attention toward spatial structure over superficial patterns

## My open question (answered)

- *"If the VLM is just pattern matching, how does wrapping it in an agent
  help?"* — Su's answer: the agent doesn't ask the VLM the end question
  directly; it externalizes the reasoning and uses the VLM only for
  targeted perceptual queries, which is what VLMs are actually good at.
