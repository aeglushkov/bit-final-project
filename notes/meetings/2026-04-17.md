## Key points

- **Not spatial-only, not temporal-only:** Su corrects my framing — the goal
  is *spatiotemporal association*, i.e., building a cognitive map that links
  sparse observations across space and time into a global understanding.
- **When vs how (refined):**
  - *When to look* = invoking the right tools at the right moment (frame
    selection / triggering).
  - *How to look* = the model's basic capabilities and information-flow
    processing (deeper than per-frame processing).
- **LongVideoAgent confirmed as a "when" method** — its contribution is
  deciding at which frames to trigger tool-based processing.
- **Neuroscience grounding:** the sparse-input-to-stable-reasoning observation
  comes from neuroscience (hippocampus). Su highlights *efficient memory
  mechanisms* as a key direction for me to explore.
- **Implicit internalization (clarified):** Su's goal is not compression in a
  lossy sense — it is making the model *internally comprehend* 60 frames worth
  of information from 30. The design may resemble a generative model
  (reconstruct full information, minimize information loss), but the purpose
  is internalization, not generation.

## Su's architecture vision (new detail)

- **Not a single VLM from scratch** — the system is a **multi-agent
  architecture** that aligns information across different foundation models.
- **Agent role:** detect when the input stream has incomplete/missing
  information, then:
  1. Call a **generative model** to reconstruct the missing information.
  2. Call **reasoning models** to refine the reconstruction.
- **Specialist sub-models:** different reconstruction models handle different
  modalities (2D, 3D, depth maps, segmentation maps), and different
  understanding models provide varying levels of interpretive power.
- **Design goal:** integrate sub-models into a "brain-like" robust system
  through architectural design.
