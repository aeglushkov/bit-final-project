**Resolution:** two separate InternVL3-8B deployments — *vanilla* InternVL3-8B
as the text planner, *spatial-intelligence-enhanced* InternVL3-8B (the variant
from *Scaling Spatial Intelligence with Multimodal Foundation Models*) as the
VLM. The split mirrors Embodied VideoAgent's original planner/VLM
decomposition; the asymmetry is intentional — Su's reasoning is that the
planner reasons over text-form tool outputs and does not need (and may even
be biased by) spatial-pretraining objectives, while the VLM is the component
that actually needs to perceive geometry.

## Key points

- **Baseline-first methodology.** Su's headline ask: before claiming any
  improvement from the agent / extended SQL / new tools, establish the
  planner's bare-VQA score on VSI-Bench so deltas are interpretable.
- **Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct VSI-Bench number Su quotes:** ~32.3% (from *Scaling
  Spatial Intelligence with Multimodal Foundation Models*). This is *higher*
  than our 30% agent result and 32% post-schema-extension result — i.e. our
  agent currently sits at or below the bare planner.
- **Su's interpretation of the gap:** Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct and InternVL2-8B
  were only pretrained for fundamental VQA, not on depth maps or other
  spatial modalities. Feeding them depth-derived information through the
  agent's tools therefore degrades, rather than helps, basic performance.
- **Reframing of the negative result as confirmation:** Su explicitly reads
  the underperformance as *proof* that "simply relying on the object memory
  mechanism of Embodied VideoAgent is insufficient for the model to capture
  spatial information" — which is exactly the limitation Su has been
  positioning as the project's optimization target since 2026-04-26.
- **Concrete recommendation (refined in follow-up):** run *two separate*
  InternVL3-8B deployments — the **vanilla** InternVL3-8B as the text
  planner, and the **spatial-intelligence-enhanced** InternVL3-8B (from the
  *Scaling Spatial Intelligence* paper) as the VLM. The asymmetry is
  intentional: the planner reasons over textual tool outputs and shouldn't
  inherit the VLM's spatial-pretraining biases, while the VLM is the
  component that actually needs to perceive geometry.

## How this connects to earlier discussions

- 2026-04-26 framing reaffirmed: the per-object memory bank of Embodied
  VideoAgent is the limitation we want to push past. Today's results are now
  treated as empirical evidence for that framing, not as a setback.
